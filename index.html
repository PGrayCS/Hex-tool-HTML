What are Hex Codes?

Hex codes are a way of representing colors using a six-digit combination of letters and numbers. They are based on the hexadecimal system, which uses 16 digits (0-9 and A-F). Each pair of digits represents the intensity of red, green, and blue light, respectively.

For example:

  • #FF0000 is red (maximum red, no green or blue)
  • #00FF00 is green (maximum green, no red or blue)
  • #0000FF is blue (maximum blue, no red or green)
  • #FFFFFF is white (maximum of all colors)
  • #000000 is black (absence of all colors)
